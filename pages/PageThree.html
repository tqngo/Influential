Under The Influence

Having somoene to look up to is always a blessing. Knowing that I have my older sister,

Trang as a role model gives me hope that I can be a great person in life. Being the

third child out of four and having an older brother, you would think that I would look up

to him, but this isn't the case. Trang is the second eldest and I can't ask for a better role

model to set an example for what life puts in front of me.


Trang influences me in a way that I could see her as my second mother. My parents

immigrated from Vietnam with little to no knowledge of the English language. Which

put Trang on a higher pedistal. She had to fill the shoes of my mom due to the huge

language barrier my mom had with America.

Trang learned best by those who influenced her the most, her parents. As a child of immigrants, she saw first hand

the hard effort it takes to provide for others, and she in turn, also wanted to give back to others by volunteering

and fundraising efforts. She worked hard to build her career. She started off by volunteering in a substance abuse

clinic, which led to being hired full-time, to being given the opportunity to grow an study to become a substance

abuse counselor. She worked in a behavioral/metal health setting for 11 years and it taught her how to be

compassionate and understanding to the different kinds of trauma people can endure.


During the pandemic, as with many others, the excess time shifted her focus to explore other career fields.

As a challenge to herself, she decided to pivot and work in education which so far she's been doing great at.
